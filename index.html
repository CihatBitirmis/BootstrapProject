Countrys

Edinburgh has been the capital of Scotland since 1437. Edinburgh, the country's second largest city after Glasgow, is located in the east of Scotland, close to the north sea. Considered one of the most beautiful cities in Europe, the city is known for its medieval and Georgian architecture.

SCOTLAND / Edinburgh

Stockholm or Stockholm is the center of the province of Stockholm and the capital of Sweden. According to 2008 data, the Stockholm metropolitan area is a center where 21% of the entire Swedish population lives and 35% of all Sweden's industry is located.

SWEDEN / Stockholm

Moscow is the capital and most populous city of Russia. It has a population of 10,406,578 in the city center. When the suburbs are added, it is home to a population approaching 18 million. Moscow is an important political, economic, cultural and scientific center of Eastern Europe.

RUSSIAN / Moscow

Istanbul is a city located in the Çatalca-Kocaeli Section of the Marmara Region in Turkey and is one of the 81 provinces of Turkey. It is the country's largest and most populated province in terms of population. It is one of the leading cities in terms of economic, historical and socio-cultural aspects.

TURKEY / Istanbul

Washington, D.C. or officially the District of Columbia, the capital of the United States. The city, also known as Washington due to its Turkish pronunciation, was named after George Washington, the first President and Founding Father of the United States. Washington was established as the seat of government for the newly independent Americans after the American Revolution.

USA / Washington DC

Tokyo, or officially Tokyo Metropolis, is the capital and one of the prefectures of Japan. Tokyo, a megalopolis area of 13,453 km² and a population of 38,505,000 million, is the largest city in the world. It is also the most expensive city to live in, according to recent research.

JAPAN / Tokyo

The flag of the United States, the flag of the United States in use. It consists of red, white and blue colors. With a total of 13 stripes, six are white and seven are red, symbolizing the Thirteen Colonies that rebelled against the United Kingdom at the time of the country's founding.

The shape of the flag of Japan, officially called Nisshōki or Hinomaru in Japanese, is a red circle on a white background. The red circle represents the sun rising from the country. The name of the country means 'the source of the sun' in its own language, and the sun is one of the most important national symbols in Japan.
